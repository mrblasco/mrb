Andrea Blasco, Karim Lakhani, Kevin Boudreau, Michael Menietti, Christoph Riedl 2013 Find a partner in the crowd (in progress)
abstract
We ran a large team-formation and matching experiment in which participants formed virtual teams of two, working together over the course of one month to develop new algorithms to locate captions and text of images of US patent drawing pages. We test whether the introduction of a centralized matching protocol based on elicited preferences can improve matching outcomes.
Andrea Blasco, Kevin Boudreau, Denisa Mindruta 2013 Have you seen the movie? Now play the game; matching in the entertainment industry (in progress)
abstract
Launching a videogame along with releasing a movie has become the preferred strategy for movie studios who aim to increase revenues from movie-related products. Understanding how film studios and video game publishers select each other for a profitable relationship is the topic of this paper. In spite of the economic relevance of video games movie tie-ins, inter--firm relationships in the industry have received very scant attention by management scholars. To the best of our knowledge, this paper is the first to systematically document and study the formation of movie/videogame industry alliances. In order to guide our empirical analysis, we propose that partnerships leading to the formation of tie-ins are the result of a competitive, two-sided matching process, where the observed winning bids are determined by the complementarity between the characteristics of the partners.
